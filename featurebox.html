Company

Four connected modules sharing a unified data layer — not a single-point tool. The architecture creates compounding defensibility: each module makes the others more accurate as more customer data flows through. Single deduction tools exist; none unify deductions, forecasting, trade analytics, and NL data access in one platform built exclusively for CPG. The unified data layer is the moat, not the individual features. Customers that start with one module consistently add more — land-and-expand built into the product architecture.

Low Risk

FeatureBox sits on top of SAP, NetSuite, and Oracle — it's the intelligence layer, not a competing system. ERPs don't serve mid-market CPG well in this category: too expensive, too slow, too generic. A more likely exit path is strategic acquisition by a CPG data company (SPINS, Nielsen) or a mid-market ERP expanding upmarket — not a kill move. The deeper risk isn't ERP competition, it's major retailers like Walmart restricting portal API access, which would increase integration maintenance costs. Worth monitoring.

Yes

CPG is a $2T+ industry. Mid-market brands ($10M–$300M revenue) represent tens of thousands of companies in the US alone — every one selling through major retailers deals with deductions and forecasting challenges. $700K+ average annual recovery with only 2 of 4 modules suggests significant NRR expansion potential as adoption deepens. The modular pricing model (scale-based, not per-seat) aligns incentives and grows with the customer. International CPG markets extend the TAM further. The question is go-to-market velocity, not market size.
